powergrade · a blank scaffold

The grade, without the grade in it.

A DaVinci Resolve PowerGrade that carries the structure of a working grade and none of the look — labelled, wired, and empty. Import it once and start every clip on a real tree. And two of its stages are compound nodes that hold a Studio-or-free choice: use a Studio feature if you have it, or combine free effects to match it.

Download the tree (.zip) ↓ .drx + .dpx · ~31 MB · MIT

Free-edition safe — every node is empty of corrections, and there are no Studio effects baked into the data (the Studio nodes are labelled placeholders). Read straight out of the .drx with Resolve's own API.

the tree

Nine stages, two of them doors.

Signal runs left to right — clean-up first, creative look last, with a foreground / background split in the middle. The Noise Reduction and Look stages are compound nodes (the stacked ones): double-click them in Resolve and they open into a small Studio-or-free tree. Tap any node here to read what it holds.

what's inside the two compound nodes

The paywall, undone in two places.

This is the whole idea, in miniature. Resolve's best cleanup and film-look tools are Studio-only. So the two stages that would reach for them are built as compound nodes with both paths inside: a slot for the Studio feature if you own it, and the free combination that matches it if you don't. Nothing is applied — every node is an empty, labelled slot. Double-click the stage in Resolve to open it.

install it once

Import as a PowerGrade.

  1. Unzip — and keep both files together. You get a .drx (the grade) and a .dpx (its thumbnail). Resolve reads the grade from one and the still from the other; the import fails if they're not in the same folder.
  2. Open the Gallery on the Color page. Color page → Gallery → open the album list on the left.
  3. Right-click a PowerGrade album → Import. Pick the .drx. The still lands in the album.
  4. Apply it to a clip. Drag the still onto a clip, or right-click it → Apply Grade. The whole node tree appears on the clip, ready to build on. Double-click Noise Reduction or Look to step inside the compound.

Imports and applies in the free edition — the Studio slots are empty and off, so nothing errors on a tool you can't run.

why a blank tree

Structure is the part worth sharing.

Looks don't travel — they're tied to a camera, a timeline colour space, a shot. Structure does. A good grade almost always ends up with the same skeleton; shipping that skeleton, empty, saves you rebuilding it on every project and starting each clip staring at a single blank node.

pay-optional, by node Studio or free, your call.

The two stages that would reach for Studio-only tools — noise reduction and the film look — hold both options inside. Own Studio? Flip on Studio NR or Film Look Creator. Don't? The free path is right there, labelled, and it lands in the same place. That's the whole project, in two nodes.

clean early, reconstruct late The two ends are the suite.

Noise Reduction sits first, Look sits last — and those are exactly where Hush and Speak plug in. Take the noise out at the top of the tree; put the optical character back at the bottom. The scaffold is shaped around that order on purpose.

the split, pre-wired Subject and world, apart.

The FG / BG parallel branch is the move most grades reach for eventually — key the subject, grade it separately from its background, recombine. It's here from the start so you add a window, not a whole node structure, when the shot needs it.

the honest notes

the 31 MB It's the thumbnail, not the grade.

The grade is a 160 KB .drx. The size is the uncompressed 4K .dpx still, which barely zips — an HD export would be a fraction of it.

colour space Grades are space-dependent.

This one carries no corrections, so there's nothing to mistranslate on import — but keep the look you build on it matched to your timeline's colour space.
