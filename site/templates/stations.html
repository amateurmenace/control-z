Built with a station, for stations.

control-z comes out of a working community media center — every tool is tested on real member workflows at Brookline Interactive Group before release. The suite is designed for the realities of access television: donated machines, volunteer editors, submission queues, tape vaults, and accessibility obligations — all on the free version of Resolve, so a lab of ten seats costs zero dollars in licenses.

What a station gets today

  • Hush — member footage shot in dark rooms stops looking like member footage.
  • Scribe — captions for access compliance and speaker-labeled markers for meeting coverage, all local.
  • Clear — the HVAC hum, the podium PA, the gym reverb: rescued. Loudness normalized to broadcast spec.
  • Pivot — every show becomes Shorts/Reels without re-editing.
  • Rise + Depth + Stencil — the archive upscales, the graders grade, nobody buys Mocha.

Where this is going

The roadmap is station-shaped: Level (drag-and-drop submission QC), Strobe (free photosensitivity checking — the test that currently costs thousands a year), Minutes (civic meetings → chapters, speakers, clip lists), Babel (language access), Rewind (the tape vault). If your station would pilot any of these, we want to hear from you.

Deploying in a lab

  1. Install free Resolve + Hush on every seat (the installer handles OFX placement; clear Resolve's plugin cache on upgrades).
  2. Standalone tools are one folder per app — no admin rights needed to run, CLI-scriptable for batch workflows (scribe-cli transcribe *.mov after every meeting, for instance).
  3. Models download once per machine into a shared store, license shown, hash verified. Air-gapped labs can copy the store folder.

Training curricula and a submission-QC playbook are being written alongside the tools. Alliance for Community Media folks: come say hi.
