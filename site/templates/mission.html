why this exists

Undo the paywall.

Blackmagic gives everyone a world-class editor for free — and gates the finishing. Noise reduction, AI masks, transcription, voice isolation, retiming: the features that turn footage into television live behind a $295 key, and the third-party market prices the rest at hundreds to thousands of dollars a seat. If you cut for a living at a post house, that's a business expense. If you run a community media center, file stories as a freelancer, or make art after work, it's a wall.

control-z is the undo button for that wall. Free, open-source tools that do the paywalled jobs — built to run with the free version of DaVinci Resolve, on the machines communities actually have.

The covenant

Every tool in the suite promises five things:

  1. Free forever, MIT-licensed. Commercial use included. No tiers, no trials, no accounts.
  2. Works with the free version of Resolve. That's the whole point.
  3. Local only. Your footage and audio never leave your machine. No cloud, no telemetry.
  4. Shows its work. Scopes, measurements, confidence maps, residual monitors — paid tools are black boxes; ours are glass.
  5. Honest limitations. Every tool page names what the paid tool still does better. Trust is the product.

Part of the Community AI Project

control-z is the post-production wing of the Community AI Project — a coalition of Weird Machine, Brookline Interactive Group, and Neighborhood AI building AI that runs locally, serves neighborhoods, and stays free. The same principles govern our models: permissively licensed weights only, downloaded transparently with a license card, verified by hash, running on your hardware.

Every tool is proven on real community media workflows — city meetings, tape archives, interview edits — at Brookline Interactive Group before it ships.
